
Working Title: Little Wars
Genre: Turn-based Strategy + Real-time Tactics (Hybrid)
Target Session Length: 90–120 minutes per campaign (with longer map options available)
Aesthetic: Retro DOS-era style (limited vibrant color palette, low resolution, pixel fonts, oblique/overhead view similar to Dungeon Keeper or Shadow of the Horned Rat, MIDI-style music)
Core Vision
A relatively short and accessible medieval strategy game focused on conquering territory through a mix of strategic decision-making, optional castle building, and tactical real-time battles. The game should feel like a spiritual successor to Castles II and Lords of the Realm, but with better combat and less frustrating management systems.
Core Fantasy:
“I am a medieval lord expanding my power through conquest, smart positioning, and consolidating my gains with castles.”
Game Structure

Overworld Layer: Turn-based map divided into regions. The player starts with at least one region. Resources are gathered and armies are moved here.
Zoomed Layer: Real-time oblique/overhead view used for both castle building and combat.
When two armies meet on the map, the game transitions into the real-time combat view.

Management Layer

Resources generate passively based on controlled territory (avoiding heavy per-county micromanagement like Lords of the Realm).
Castles can “fortify” surrounding regions, reducing revolt risk and increasing resource production (inspired by Castles II).
The player makes high-level strategic decisions rather than managing individual counties.

Castle Building

Players can design their own castles or use preset layouts (similar to the original Castles).
Buildings include walls, gates, towers, moats, and a keep.
Castles are rewarding but optional. A player should be able to win through aggressive conquest without building many, but castles should make long-term control easier and more stable.

Combat System

Real-time with pause, group-based control.
Small unit counts: 4–8 groups per side.
Each “unit” represents a group of soldiers (roughly 10–15 men, or ~5 for knights).
Player controls groups via flags (not individual soldiers).
Strong emphasis on unit composition, positioning, and terrain.
Defender gets a setup phase before battle.
Player can pause at any time to issue new orders.
Auto-battle option available.
Battles should be significantly shorter than Total War battles.

Example Combat Flow (from user):
The player uses terrain (hill + bog), positions archers defensively with infantry screening, focuses fire, and uses knights for flanking. The goal is to reward smart play and positioning, allowing a smaller force to defeat a larger one through better tactics.
Desired Order Types:

Hold Position
Attack Target
Move to Location
Possibly “Defend Area”

Strong retro DOS aesthetic
Limited but vibrant color palette
Oblique/overhead perspective

Target Experience

Approachable but not simplistic
Strategic depth without overwhelming complexity
Satisfying combat that rewards positioning and composition
Shorter, more focused campaigns than modern grand strategy games
Castle building as a meaningful strategic tool, not mandatory
